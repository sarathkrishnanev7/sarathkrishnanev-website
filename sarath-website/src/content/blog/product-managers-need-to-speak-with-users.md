One of the stranger patterns in product is how often product managers avoid speaking directly to users. Mostly it is because user conversations feel expensive - time, ambiguity and disruption.

Data on the other hand gives you patterns - and lets you derive inferences based on your internal bias. User conversations introduce context, contradiction and discomfort. Sometimes they tell you the feature you spent months building does not matter. Sometimes they reveal that the problem you thought you were solving was never the real problem.

That is hard to hear, but that is also where product clarity comes from.

I keep coming back to a phrase: **deploy empathy**. Not as a soft skill, but as a product skill. There is also a book by the same name — *Deploy Empathy* — which I think is a must-read for product managers. I have not finished it myself yet, but even what I have read so far has been reassuring. Especially if you are struggling to figure out how to engage users meaningfully, ask better questions or reduce ambiguity in product decisions.

The more distance you create from users, the more assumptions fill the gap. And assumptions are expensive.
